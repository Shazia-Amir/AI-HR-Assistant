# Office Hours & Work Policy

## Overview

This document defines the working hours, attendance policies, remote work guidelines, and time tracking requirements for all employees.

## Working Hours

- **Standard Hours**: 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM (IST), Monday to Friday.
- **Total Hours**: 45 hours per week (9 hours per day including 1 hour lunch break).
- **Core Hours**: 10:30 AM to 4:30 PM — all employees must be available during core hours unless on approved leave.
- **Grace Period**: 15 minutes grace period for arrival (up to 9:15 AM).
- **Lunch Break**: 1 hour, typically between 12:30 PM and 2:00 PM.

## Flexible Hours

- **Flexi-Time Window**: Employees may arrive between 8:00 AM and 10:30 AM, with corresponding adjustment to departure time.
- **Requirement**: Must complete 8 working hours per day (excluding breaks).
- **Approval**: Flexible hours must be approved by the immediate manager.
- **Core Hours Compliance**: Even with flexible hours, core hours (10:30 AM - 4:30 PM) are mandatory.

## Attendance

- **Marking Attendance**: Employees must mark attendance using the biometric system or HR portal upon arrival and departure.
- **Missed Attendance**: If attendance is not marked, employees must submit a manual attendance request within 24 hours.
- **Minimum Attendance**: 90% attendance is required per month. Anything below this requires HR review.
- **Half Day**: An employee working less than 5 hours is considered on half-day leave.

## Time Tracking

- **Tool**: All employees must log time using the company's time tracking tool (Jira/Timesheet portal).
- **Daily Logging**: Time must be logged daily by end of day.
- **Weekly Review**: Managers review time logs every Monday for the previous week.
- **Project Codes**: Time must be logged against appropriate project codes.
- **Non-Project Time**: Meetings, training, and admin work must be logged under respective non-project codes.

## Remote Work

- **Eligibility**: Employees who have completed 3 months of employment and whose role permits remote work.
- **Maximum Days**: Up to 8 remote working days per month.
- **Request**: Remote work must be requested and approved at least 1 day in advance through the HR portal.
- **Availability**: Employees working remotely must be available on company communication tools (Slack, Email, Phone) during working hours.
- **Deliverables**: Remote work days require a summary of completed tasks submitted to the manager.
- **Equipment**: Employees must use company-provided laptops and VPN for remote work.

## Hybrid Work

- **Model**: 3 days in office, 2 days remote per week (default hybrid model).
- **Office Days**: Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday are mandatory office days.
- **Remote Days**: Monday and Friday are default remote days.
- **Customization**: Teams may adjust hybrid schedules with manager and HR approval.
- **Team Coordination**: All team members must be in the office on the same days for collaboration.

## Work From Home

- **WFH vs Remote**: Work From Home (WFH) is for occasional needs, while Remote Work is a planned arrangement.
- **WFH Allowance**: Up to 4 WFH days per month for unforeseen circumstances (internet issues, personal emergencies, minor illness).
- **Notification**: WFH must be communicated to the manager before 9:00 AM on the WFH day.
- **Frequency**: WFH cannot be used for more than 2 consecutive days without HR approval.

## Late Policy

- **Late Arrival**: Arriving after 9:15 AM is considered late.
- **Late Count**: More than 3 late arrivals per month triggers a warning.
- **Half-Day Leave**: Arriving after 11:30 AM will result in a half-day leave deduction.
- **Escalation**: 
  - 1st instance: Verbal warning from manager.
  - 2nd instance: Written warning from HR.
  - 3rd instance: Meeting with HR and Department Head.
  - 4th instance: May impact performance review.
- **Exception**: Late arrivals due to genuine emergencies (documented) will be excused.

## Break Rules

- **Lunch Break**: 1 hour (12:30 PM - 2:00 PM window).
- **Tea/Coffee Breaks**: Two 15-minute breaks per day (mid-morning and mid-afternoon).
- **Total Break Time**: Must not exceed 1.5 hours per day (including lunch).
- **Break Logging**: Breaks are not required to be logged but should not disrupt work continuity.
- **Smoke Breaks**: Smoking is permitted only in designated areas. Excessive smoke breaks (more than 4 per day) may be addressed by HR.

## Overtime

- **Definition**: Any work done beyond 9 hours per day or on weekends/holidays.
- **Prior Approval**: Overtime must be pre-approved by the manager.
- **Compensation**: 
  - Weekday overtime: Compensatory off (1 hour OT = 1 hour comp-off, minimum 4 hours to qualify).
  - Weekend/holiday work: Compensatory off (1 day work = 1 day comp-off).
- **Comp-Off Usage**: Comp-off must be used within 60 days of being earned.
- **Maximum Overtime**: 12 hours of overtime per week.
- **Logging**: All overtime must be logged in the time tracking tool with manager approval.
- **Comp-Off Encashment**: Unused comp-offs cannot be encashed and expire after 60 days.
