# Leave Policy

## Overview

This document outlines the comprehensive leave policy for all employees of the company. It covers all types of leaves, eligibility criteria, application procedures, and approval workflows.

## Annual Leave

- **Entitlement**: 20 days per calendar year for full-time employees.
- **Accrual**: Leave accrues monthly at a rate of 1.67 days per month.
- **Eligibility**: All full-time employees who have completed their probation period (3 months).
- **Booking**: Annual leave must be requested at least 7 days in advance through the HR portal.
- **Carry Forward**: Up to 5 unused annual leave days can be carried forward to the next calendar year. Any remaining unused leave will be forfeited.
- **Encashment**: Unused annual leave cannot be encashed during employment. Terminal leave encashment is available upon resignation subject to company policy.

## Sick Leave

- **Entitlement**: 10 days per calendar year.
- **Accrual**: Sick leave accrues monthly at a rate of 0.83 days per month.
- **Eligibility**: All full-time and part-time employees.
- **Documentation**: A medical certificate from a registered medical practitioner is required for sick leave exceeding 2 consecutive days.
- **Notification**: Employees must notify their manager and HR within the first 2 hours of the workday.
- **Carry Forward**: Unused sick leave is not carried forward to the next year.
- **Encashment**: Sick leave cannot be encashed.

## Casual Leave

- **Entitlement**: 5 days per calendar year.
- **Purpose**: For personal errands, family events, or short personal commitments.
- **Notification**: Casual leave must be requested at least 1 day in advance, except in emergencies.
- **Carry Forward**: Casual leave is not carried forward.
- **Encashment**: Casual leave cannot be encashed.

## Emergency Leave

- **Entitlement**: 2 days per calendar year.
- **Purpose**: For unforeseen emergencies such as accidents, sudden illness, or urgent family matters.
- **Notification**: Employees must inform their manager as soon as possible, ideally before the start of the workday.
- **Documentation**: May be required depending on the nature of the emergency.
- **Carry Forward**: Emergency leave is not carried forward.

## Maternity Leave

- **Entitlement**: 26 weeks (approximately 180 days) of paid maternity leave.
- **Eligibility**: Female employees who have completed at least 80 working days in the 12 months preceding the expected delivery date.
- **Application**: Must be submitted at least 8 weeks before the expected delivery date with a medical certificate.
- **Split**: Maternity leave can be split into pre-natal and post-natal periods as per the employee's preference.
- **Return to Work**: Employees returning from maternity leave are entitled to flexible working hours for the first 2 months.
- **Nursing Breaks**: Two nursing breaks of 30 minutes each are provided per day until the child is 15 months old.

## Paternity Leave

- **Entitlement**: 2 weeks (14 days) of paid paternity leave.
- **Eligibility**: Male employees whose spouse has given birth or who have adopted a child.
- **Application**: Must be submitted within 30 days of the child's birth or adoption.
- **Purpose**: To support the spouse and bond with the new child.
- **Usage**: Must be taken consecutively and cannot be split.

## Bereavement Leave

- **Entitlement**: 3 days of paid bereavement leave.
- **Eligibility**: All employees.
- **Purpose**: In the event of the death of an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, sibling, or grandparent).
- **Extended Bereavement**: 5 days if the deceased is a spouse or child.
- **Notification**: Employees must inform their manager and HR as soon as possible.
- **Documentation**: A death certificate may be requested by HR.

## Public Holidays

- **Entitlement**: 10 paid public holidays per calendar year.
- **List of Holidays**:
  1. New Year's Day (January 1)
  2. Republic Day (January 26)
  3. Holi (March, date varies)
  4. Good Friday (March/April, date varies)
  5. Independence Day (August 15)
  6. Gandhi Jayanti (October 2)
  7. Dussehra (October, date varies)
  8. Diwali (October/November, date varies)
  9. Christmas Day (December 25)
  10. Company Foundation Day (date varies)
- **Floating Holiday**: Employees may choose 1 additional floating holiday per year from a list of regional holidays.

## Carry Forward Policy

- Only annual leave can be carried forward, up to a maximum of 5 days.
- Carry forward must be used by March 31st of the following year.
- Any carry-forward leave not used by March 31st will be forfeited.
- Leave cannot be carried forward if the employee is serving notice period.

## Leave Approval Workflow

1. **Employee** submits a leave request through the HR portal.
2. **Immediate Manager** reviews and approves/rejects the request within 2 working days.
3. **HR Department** is automatically notified of approved leaves.
4. **Employee** receives an email notification of the decision.
5. If the **Manager** rejects the request, the employee may appeal to the Department Head within 3 working days.
6. **Department Head** decision is final.
7. All approved leaves are automatically reflected in the attendance system.

### Leave Cancellation

- Employees can cancel approved leave requests through the HR portal.
- Cancellations must be made at least 1 day before the leave start date.
- Cancelled leaves are credited back to the employee's leave balance.
